Marsh Creek Trail

The Wilderness in Our Backyard

By Arianna Velasco

The Trail

The Marsh Creek Trail is more than simply a path following a stream, its an entire ecosystem filled with amazing scenery available to the thousands of residents of Brentwood and Oakley. Some are only a fence hop away from it.

The trail runs through the back of houses, gas stations, parks etc.. It is essentially embedded among city and nature. Signs are commonly used around the trail to guide the biker/hiker on where to go. They also show where someone can enter or exit the trail from their nearest location. The terrain varies from smooth sidewalk, to rough gravel and even bridges where the one can enjoy a beautiful sunset.

Location

The Marsh Creek Trail covers approxmately 14 miles of land. It begins at the foothills of Morgan Territory Regional Preserve and goes all the way to the Delta shores in Oakley.It passes through the City of Brentwood, farm fields in the borders of Oakley, Brentwood and Knightsen, The City of Oakley and flows into the Delta.

The Marsh Creek Trail gives its specatators gorgeous views of the area. Hikers have access to multiple spots that give great views of Mt. Diablo, the tallest mountain in the surrounding Area. One can also see the crops which vary from corn to peaches growing next to the creek.

The Creek

The Creek begins on the eastern side of Mt. Diablo and ends in the banks of the Delta. However it makes quite a journey before it reaches its end. The river first touches a city at Brentwood where the trail begins. It goes through houses, Parks and finally recahes the Marsh Creek Resevoir where poeple fish and wade in the water. It goes throught the farms of Brentwood to the neighborhoods in Oakley. Finally, it goes to the ends of Oakley into the Delta. However the Creek is a home to many creatures and is a nice complement to the trail.

Wildlife

Previous Next

While not seen at plain sight, when one looks closely the can see the river full of life. Fish flow alongside tadpoles throughout the Creek, brinnging fishers from all ages to the creeks bank. Turtles soak the sun from early morning to late afternoon and hide from anyone who comes to close. Butterflies and dragonflies fly across the plants on the boarders of the creeks and occasisionaly draw in diffrent species of birds to the banks. Wolly Bears race across the trail to get to the branches; squirrels rest under the shade of a bush and lizards scram across the rocks that run down the creek. This creek is full of life.

Come Visit

The Park is open to the public and most areas allow bikes, and even horses. With multiple entrence points there is no need to start at the beginnig to end, just look up the closest near you. Be sure to bring water and stay hydrated, the weather can get up to the 100's F. Sunscreen is essential if you go out in the middle of the day. However with all of the preparations made this trail can be a great a great experience whether you go with family, friends or on your own.
